Meet the Founder

Tamaki Hoshi

Founder & Lead Writer

"From back-alley greengrocers to third-generation hyogu craftsmen, people lead so many intriguing lives across Japan. My mission is to craft those life's tales into lasting stories."

Background

Tamaki Hoshi is a Tokyo-based writer and historian devoted to uncovering untold stories in Japan, especially those of women. Raised in the United States, she returned to Tokyo in 2019 to pursue higher education at Waseda University, where she earned her MA in Social Sciences in 2024 with a focus on women's history.

Beyond academia, Tamaki blends scholarship with storytelling. She has contributed articles to platforms like Voyapon and Metropolis Japan, where her writing explores how aesthetics, traditions, and everyday customs shape Japanese life—past and present. She has also written and illustrated cultural storybooks that preserve heritage for younger audiences and is currently developing a novel of Japanese historical fiction.

Her public engagement extends to her role as Miss World Japan 2021, where she represented Japan internationally in Puerto Rico and worked on promotional projects for local tourism and community regeneration. She continues to share insights on Japanese culture through lectures, essays, and social media content, often weaving together academic rigor with accessible narratives.

Print Publications

Nature's Promise: A Baby Carrot's Journey

Storybook | 2020

A self-published storybook addressing the social issue of food waste. The story follows a baby carrot on a journey to fulfill nature's promise by passing on his stored energy to another living being—but his purpose is cut short when he's discarded uneaten. Aimed at children, the book introduces the mottainai mindset, encouraging awareness of food waste not just as a practical concern, but as a moral and environmental issue.

Remembering Mothers

Anthology | 2023

A chapter contribution to a Japanese anthology titled Remembering Mothers (『あらためていま母を想う』). In my memoir-styled chapter, I reflect on my mother's inspiring journey raising me in the United States as a non-native English speaker.

Legends of Bajo Mola

Storybook | 2022

A co-edited storybook based on oral legends. I participated in an urban regeneration project in the Indonesian village of Bajo Mola, where we collaborated with local residents to preserve their intangible cultural heritage by documenting the oral legends of the Bajo people. I contributed both the illustrations and one of the narratives to the three featured stories.
